Dr. Erin Murphy

University of Michigan, BS
Wayne State University, MD

My favorite things to do outside work:

hiking/backpacking, snowboarding, mountain biking, snowshoeing, kayaking, disc golf, ultimate frisbee- basically anything outdoors. I also enjoy reading, indoor rock climbing, watching Tigers games and University of Michigan football, attending local concerts, traveling to Alaska, gazing at snow-capped mountains, and hanging out with my boyfriend and my 9-year old yellow lab, Syd.

Why I chose Receiving/DRH:

I love Detroit! There's tons of fun stuff to do here: great music and art scene, sports teams, restaurants and bars with delicious food and beer, Eastern Market, the riverfront, new parks and bike paths. Detroit's coming back. And I'm not just saying that, I've been here 8 years and have seen a lot of positive change. This city has heart. Detroit Receiving is an amazing place to train. I chose DRH because I loved the patient population, the personalities of the residents and the superb leadership and teaching of the diverse faculty. I also wanted to be worked hard during residency so that I would feel comfortable working basically anywhere afterwards.

Dr. Timothy Scott

University of Toledo, BS
Lincoln Memorial University- DeBusk College of Osteopathic Medicine, DO

Why I chose Receiving:

Now that I got that out of the way ... I love being an EM resident!!! (but life may be slightly better once I am an attending). DRH has been a blessing. If I could go back and do it all over, I'd change nothing. My colleagues are awesome and the training is second to none. Detroit is a blast too! I live in a rebuilt victorian-home-turned-townhouse in Midtown and it is amazing. I am close to all the museums, tons of restaurants, Lions, Tigers and Beers ... oh my!! When I am not at work I am reading, playing with my dog, listening to Mariah Carey (guess my favorite song and I'll sing it to you), working out or having a cold one with friends.
